Saving Students Money

We aim to advocate for students by lowering the cost of attending UIC by reducing fees, making more grants and scholarships available, and by advocating to create free textbook & I-clicker programs.

Free Textbook Programs

- Expanding on the OER (Open Source Textbook) program

- Use data collected by IllinoisPIRG to justify creating free book programs

Addressing Fees and Tuition Prices for Online Classes

- Working to reduce or eliminate fees for service that students cannot or will not utilize

- Researching the differences in effectiveness of online learning and the value lost

$15/hr Minimum Wage for student employees

We also aim to increase the financial value gained by attending UIC by fighting for a $15/hr on-campus minimum wage, and addressing the lack of tuition change when classes moved online.

- By working with University leadership we plan to leverage student leadership to urge the administration to make this much needed change to $15/hr.

Prioritizing Mental Health

We aim to increase the amount of mental health resources at UIC due to the heightened anxiety caused by online learning and the Covid-19 pandemic. By urging administration to hire more counselors with Master's degrees instead of limiting their search for Ph.D candidates, introducing much needed crisis counselors for those experiencing panic and or anxiety attacks, improving outreach, and making more mental health workshops available to all students.


- We plan to make these resources intertwined with the UIC Safe App for ease of student access.

Providing a Students-First Pandemic Response

We aim to ensure a safe shift back to in-person learning, by having free masks and sanitizer across campus. We also aim to ensure the repeated sanitization of populated areas and open up campus for proper social distancing. It is our mission to ensure that what is best for UIC students is at the core of pandemic response policy.


- Use student input for improving hybrid and online learning

- Open campus for social distancing

Empowering Student Clubs and Organizations

As the current Treasurer of USG Anshu’s role often requires him to meet with E-boards of many different clubs. I aim to continue my work of maintaining long mutually beneficial relationships among USG and clubs on campus.


- We aim to fix the funding structure so clubs who truly need funding receive it.

- Plan to work with the Center for Student Involvement to improve relationships between USG and student organizations and clubs.
